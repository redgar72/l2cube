Cross

Solving the white cross efficiently

The cross is the foundation of CFOP solving. A well-executed cross should be completed in 8 moves or fewer, typically without rotations. This section covers various cross techniques with a focus on rotationless methods.

Cross Fundamentals

Before diving into advanced techniques, understand these key principles:

  • Efficiency: Aim for 8 moves or fewer
  • Rotationless: Avoid cube rotations during cross
  • Planning: Plan your entire cross during inspection
  • Color neutrality: Consider solving cross on any color

Basic Cross Technique

Start with the simplest approach: solve one edge at a time.

Daisy Method

A beginner-friendly approach that creates a "daisy" pattern first, then converts it to a cross.

Step 1: Create the Daisy

Move all white edges to the top layer with white facing up, forming a daisy pattern.

Step 2: Convert to Cross

For each edge, align it with its center and do F2 to insert it.

Advanced Cross Techniques

Cross + 1

Solve the cross and one corner simultaneously. This is a key skill for advanced solvers.

Case 1: Corner in UFR

When white corner is in UFR with white on top:

R U R' (cross edge) + U R U' R' (corner)

Case 2: Corner in UFL

When white corner is in UFL with white on top:

L' U' L (cross edge) + U' L' U L (corner)

X-Cross

Solve the cross and the first F2L pair simultaneously. This is an advanced technique that can save significant time.

X-Cross Example

Cross: R U R' F' U F

F2L Pair: U R U' R'

Total: R U R' F' U F U R U' R'

Cross Planning

Advanced solvers plan their entire cross during the 15-second inspection time.

Planning Tips

  • Start with the hardest edge: Look for edges that require the most moves
  • Consider move cancellations: Plan moves that can cancel with each other
  • Look for pairs: Some edges can be solved together efficiently
  • Practice with a timer: Use inspection time effectively

Planning Example

Given this scramble: R U R' F' U F U R U' R'

Planned solution: R U R' F' U F (6 moves)

Color Neutral Cross

Solving the cross on any color can give you better starting positions.

Benefits of Color Neutrality

  • Better crosses: Some colors naturally have easier crosses
  • More options: Choose the best starting position
  • Reduced rotations: Avoid cube rotations more easily

Common Cross Patterns

Recognize these common patterns for faster cross solving:

Line Pattern

Two edges form a line on the top face

Solution: F R U R' U' F'

L-Pattern

Two edges form an L-shape on the top face

Solution: F U R U' R' F'

Dot Pattern

No edges are solved on the top face

Solution: F R U R' U' F' U2 F R U R' U' F'

Cross Solving Tips

  • Practice inspection: Use the full 15 seconds to plan your cross
  • Look ahead: While solving the cross, look for your first F2L pair
  • Finger tricks: Learn efficient finger tricks for cross moves
  • Color neutrality: Consider learning to solve on multiple colors
  • Cross + 1: Practice solving cross and one corner together
  • X-cross: Advanced technique to solve cross and first F2L pair
